The History of Barber Surgery

Barber surgeons used to perform surgeries in medieval Europe- often helping to treat people injured by the war.

Promotion in the field of barber surgery came from apprenticeship, not academia. Many of these practitioners were illiterate.

Tasks include bloodletting, removing teeth, performing enemas and small-scale surgery as well as cutting hair.

The symbol of the modern barber pole comes from a bloody cloth wrapped around a cylinder.

This object was placed outside of the barber shop to advertise that the barber inside could also perform surgery.

Barber pole technology revolves around the work of William "Bill" Marvy.

He began working at a barber supply company in 1922 at the age of 13 years old.

On New Year's Day in 1950, the Marvy family came up with our current image of the barber pole.

William Marvy Company continues to make barber poles, specializing in products to clean them.

The company, located in St. Paul, Minnesota, has produced over 82,000 barber poles.
